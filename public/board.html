Governance Roles

Two ways to serve.

HMC has two distinct governance bodies. Both play a meaningful role in the organization's direction, accountability, and community connection.

Governing Body

Board of Directors

The Board is HMC's governing body, responsible for strategic direction, fiduciary oversight, and safeguarding the organization's mission and assets. Directors carry legal duties of care, loyalty, and obedience to the organization.

3-year term, typically renewable once consecutively

Fiduciary oversight of financial and operational integrity

Quarterly meetings — first Monday of January, April, July, and October

Committee participation across seven standing committees

Executive selection and oversight of the chief executive

Governing · Fiduciary · Strategic

Advisory Body

Community Advisory Board

The CAB elevates lived experience and community priorities to inform HMC's programs, policy positions, and quality improvement. CAB recommendations are non-binding but are formally recorded and considered by the Board and management.

2-year term, renewable

Lived-experience input shaping programs and community engagement

Quarterly meetings — third Monday of March, June, September, and December

Advisory, not binding — your voice shapes decisions without board-level liability

Working groups aligned to strategic initiatives and community needs

Advisory · Community Voice · Accessible

Time Commitment

Service that fits your life.

Board and advisory service at HMC is designed to be accessible. Meetings are quarterly, materials arrive a week in advance, and committee work is structured around your availability.

Quarterly full-board meetings — held virtually or hybrid; evenings to accommodate work schedules

Agendas and materials distributed at least 7 days in advance so you can prepare on your own time

Committee participation — monthly or as needed; committees meet in focused, 60-minute sessions

Ambassadorial duties — champion HMC's mission through your existing networks, at your own pace

No mandatory give-get — financial contributions are welcomed but not required at this time

What You Gain

Serve with purpose.
Lead with impact.

Board and advisory service is a two-way commitment. HMC provides governance members with the tools, information, and access they need to serve effectively.

Governance Portal Access

Full access to the HMC Volunteer Portal with a dedicated governance view — meeting materials, committee documents, training modules, and org calendar in one place.

Meeting Materials and Minutes

Agendas, board packets, financial reports, and approved minutes delivered through the portal at least 7 days before each meeting.

Committee Participation

Join one or more of seven standing committees aligned to your expertise — from finance and governance to programs, workforce, and community engagement.

Governance Training

Required and optional training modules through the portal's Training Academy, including board governance, ethics, conflict of interest, and sector-specific resources.

Real Strategic Impact

Your votes and advisory input directly shape HMC's programs, partnerships, and resource allocation — meaningful influence on health equity strategy in Los Angeles.

D&O Insurance Coverage

Board members are covered under HMC's Directors and Officers liability insurance policy, providing protection for good-faith decisions made in the course of board service.

Onboarding Process

What Happens After
You Apply.

From application to first meeting, here is exactly what to expect — and when.

01

Application Review

Your application is reviewed within 5–7 business days. Our team evaluates your background, skills, and alignment with current board needs.

02

Invitation & Orientation

If selected, you receive a formal invitation and are scheduled for a 30-minute orientation call with the Executive Director.

03

Portal Access

Your board governance account is activated in the volunteer portal. You can access meeting materials, documents, and committee assignments immediately.

04

Document Signing

Complete three required governance documents in the portal: Conflict of Interest Disclosure, Confidentiality Agreement, and Code of Conduct. All digital, no printing needed.

05

First Meeting

Attend your first quarterly board meeting. Materials are delivered 7 days in advance. Committee placement confirmed at or before this meeting.
