Training Guide

A step-by-step walkthrough for core volunteers — from logging in for the first time to adding clients, completing wellness questionnaires, and checking people in on event day.

Step 1

Logging In

Access the portal at volunteer.healthmatters.clinic. You can sign in with your Google account or the email and password you used when you applied.

1
Go to the portal
Open your browser and navigate to volunteer.healthmatters.clinic. Bookmark it — you'll be coming back often.
2
Sign in with Google (recommended)
Click Continue with Google and select the email address associated with your HMC volunteer account. This is the fastest option and doesn't require a password.
3
Or sign in with email + password
Enter the email you used when you applied. If you've forgotten your password, click Forgot password to reset it.
4
You're in — land on your Dashboard
After login you'll see your personal dashboard. If you're brand new, it will prompt you to complete orientation first. If you're already approved, you'll see your stats, upcoming missions, and action items.
✓
First time? If you see an onboarding screen instead of your dashboard, you'll need to complete Tier 1 orientation in the Training Academy before anything else unlocks. Jump to Step 3 — Training Academy.

Step 3

Training Academy

All volunteers must complete training before they can sign up for missions. The Training Academy is organized into tiers — you unlock each tier as you progress.

1
Click "Training" in the sidebar
The Training Academy tab is accessible to everyone, even during onboarding. You'll see your current progress at the top.
2
Complete Tier 1 — Orientation (required first)
Two videos you must watch and pass before anything else unlocks:

1. HMC Orientation — Watch the video, then answer the quiz questions in your own words.
2. Because You're a Champion — Same format. Takes about 10–15 minutes total.
3
Complete Tier 2 — Core Volunteer Training
Once Tier 1 is done and your role is approved, Tier 2 unlocks. Three required modules:

HIPAA Non-Clinical — Read the document and click Acknowledge.
Community Mental Health Workshop — Video + quiz.
Survey Data Collection — Video + quiz. This covers how to enter client data correctly.
4
Complete program-specific training (if required)
For Street Medicine Outreach, you may see an additional SMO training module. Complete this before your first SMO event. It covers field protocols and safety.
⚠
Can't RSVP to a mission? The most common reason is incomplete training. Go to Training Academy and look for any module showing "In Progress" or a lock icon. Complete those first, then try registering again.
ℹ
Quiz tips: Answers are graded on key concepts, not exact wording. Write at least 2–3 sentences in your own words. You'll know you passed when the module shows a green checkmark.
Step 4

Finding Events

Once your training is complete, you can browse available missions and sign up for events that match your schedule and skills.

1
Click "My Missions" in the sidebar
This tab shows all available events, your current assignments, and your attendance history. It's your primary workspace for event management.
2
Click "Available Missions"
This shows all upcoming events you're eligible to join. Events you've already registered for are shown separately under "My Assignments."
3
Use the filters to narrow results
You can filter by category (Street Medicine, Workshop, Health Fair, etc.) or location. Events are sorted by date — soonest first.
4
Click any event card to see full details
The detail view shows the full description, location with map link, volunteer slot count (how many spots are left), required training, and the registration button.
✓
Don't see an event? Check the Calendar tab — it shows every event on the schedule. If an event you're looking for isn't showing in Available Missions, it may have a training prerequisite or limited capacity. Check with your coordinator.
Step 5

Registering for a Mission

Registering (RSVPing) to a mission adds you to the volunteer roster and sends you a confirmation. You can also add the event directly to your personal calendar.

1
Open the event and click "RSVP" or "Register"
The button label varies by event type. One click registers you — there's a brief confirmation message, then the event moves to your "My Assignments" list.
2
Add to your personal calendar
After registering, click Download ICS to add the event directly to Google Calendar, Apple Calendar, or Outlook. This creates a calendar invite with the event name, date, time, and location.
3
Check "My Assignments" to confirm
Go to My Missions → My Assignments to see your registered events. Your upcoming mission should also appear on your dashboard under "Next Mission."
4
Need to cancel? Contact your coordinator
If your plans change, let your team lead or coordinator know as soon as possible so the slot can be reassigned. Use the Communication Hub to message them directly.
Step 6

Calendar View

The Calendar tab gives you a full month-by-month view of all HMC events. It's useful for planning ahead and seeing the full schedule at a glance.

CAL
Browse by month
Use the arrow buttons to navigate forward and back by month. Days with events show colored dots — multiple dots mean multiple events that day.
CLR
Color coding
Each event type has a color — Street Medicine (rose), Wellness (emerald), Workshop (violet), Health Fair (sky), Training (green). Use the filter dropdown to show one type at a time.
TAP
Click a day
Clicking any day shows all events for that date on the right. Click an event title to open its full detail panel with the RSVP button.
RSVP
RSVP from calendar
You can register directly from the calendar detail panel — same as the My Missions tab. The event will appear in your assignments immediately.
Step 7 — Core Workflow

Adding a Client

At outreach events, you'll add clients to the HMC system using the Client Intake form. This captures the information we need to connect them with services and track our community impact.

⚠
Before you begin: This section of the portal is accessible on event day through your dashboard or the Client Intake tab. Make sure your device is charged and connected to the internet or the event's WiFi before starting.
1
Navigate to Client Intake
In the portal sidebar, look for Client Intake. On event day, a shortcut may also appear on your dashboard under "Active Mission Tools."
2
Click "Add New Client"
This opens a blank intake form. Each client gets their own form — never add multiple clients on one form.
3
Fill in the client's basic information
Collect what the client is comfortable sharing. Required fields are marked — everything else is optional but helpful.
4
Submit the form
Click Save Client or Submit at the bottom of the form. You'll see a confirmation and the client record will be created. Then proceed to the Wellness Questionnaire.

Client Intake — Field Reference

What to collect and why. Required fields must be completed to save the record.

Field Status Notes
First Name Required First name or preferred name is fine
Last Name Required If client prefers to keep private, note that in comments
Date of Birth Required Used for age-based service eligibility
Phone Number Optional For follow-up outreach. Ask if they'd like to be contacted
Email Address Optional For service updates and resource newsletters
ZIP Code Required Used to confirm LA County service area eligibility
Housing Status Required Housed, unstably housed, or unhoused — select closest option
Service Needs Optional Multi-select. Check all that apply: mental health, food, housing, medical, etc.
How did they hear about us? Optional Referral source tracking — helps us understand outreach effectiveness
Consent to Contact Required Must be confirmed before saving. Read the consent statement to the client
Notes Optional Anything else relevant — language preference, urgent needs, referrals made
ℹ
Privacy first: Only collect what clients are comfortable sharing. Never pressure someone to give information. If a client declines to share their name, you can record them as "Anonymous" with their ZIP code and service needs only.
Step 8 — Core Workflow

Completing the Wellness Questionnaire

After adding a client, you'll walk them through the wellness questionnaire. This is a standardized set of questions that helps us assess their current wellbeing and connect them with the right resources.

1
Open the questionnaire from the client's record
After saving the client, you'll see a Start Questionnaire or Complete Survey button. Click it to begin. The questionnaire is linked to this specific client record.
2
Read each question aloud to the client
This is a conversational process — you're the one entering responses, they're answering verbally. Speak clearly, go at their pace, and don't rush. Most questionnaires take 5–8 minutes.
3
Select or type their answers
Most questions are multiple choice or scale-based (e.g., 1–10 or Never/Sometimes/Often). A few questions may have free-text fields — type their response as they give it. Don't interpret or paraphrase.
4
Flag urgent needs if prompted
Some answers will trigger a yellow or red flag in the system (e.g., housing crisis, mental health risk). If you see a flag, notify your on-site coordinator immediately — do not try to handle this alone. The system will also show next steps.
5
Submit the questionnaire
Click Submit at the bottom. The client's record now shows the completed questionnaire and any resource recommendations generated by the system. Share those recommendations with the client before they leave.
✓
Handling sensitive questions: If a client seems uncomfortable with a question, it's okay to skip it — mark it as "Declined." Your job is to make them feel safe, not to complete a perfect form. A partial questionnaire is always better than no questionnaire.
Step 9 — Event Day

Checking In a Client

Client check-in is how we track who attended each outreach event. It's a quick process — your coordinator will confirm the workflow at the event briefing, but here's what it looks like in the portal.

1
On event day, go to the event in My Missions
Find the active event under My Assignments. On event day, it will show an Event Day badge or active status. Click it to open the event tools.
2
Search for the client by name
If the client is already in the system from a previous event, search their name to pull up their existing record. This saves time and links their check-in history.
3
If it's their first time — add them as a new client
Click New Client, complete the intake form (Step 7), then return here to finish check-in. New clients should be added before marking them as checked in.
4
Click "Check In"
Once you've found or created their record, click the Check In button. Their status will change to "Checked In" with a timestamp. The attendance counter on the event updates in real time.
5
Complete the wellness questionnaire (if not done)
If the client hasn't done a questionnaire recently, the system may prompt you to complete one now or flag it as recommended. Do this while the client is still at your station.
ℹ
Returning clients: If a returning client's information has changed — new phone number, new address, housing situation — update their record at check-in. Click Edit Client before checking them in.
⚠
Internet issues? If the portal is loading slowly on-site, try refreshing or switching to your phone's hotspot. If it's completely down, use the paper backup forms your coordinator will have on hand — all paper records get entered into the system within 24 hours.
Step 10

After the Event

Once the event wraps up, there are a few quick things to take care of in the portal. This is how your hours get logged and how we improve future events.

  • →
    Complete the post-event debrief survey. A pop-up will appear in the portal after the event closes. It takes 2–3 minutes and awards XP. Share what worked, what didn't, and any notable client interactions (without identifying information).
  • →
    Verify your hours are logged. Go to your dashboard and check your Hours Contributed stat. Hours are logged when your coordinator marks the shift complete. If they're missing after 48 hours, submit a support ticket.
  • →
    Check for any follow-up action items. If a client was flagged during the questionnaire or check-in process, your coordinator may assign you a follow-up task in the portal. Check your Action Items on the dashboard.
  • →
    Update your availability if needed. If your schedule changed, go to your Profile and update your availability so coordinators can plan future events accurately.
  • →
    Check the Communication Hub. Your coordinator or team lead may post a debrief message or next steps in the Briefing tab. Check it within 24 hours after the event.

Common Issues — Quick Fixes

Problem Fix
Can't RSVP to an event Check Training Academy — you likely have an incomplete module. Complete it, then try again.
Google login not showing Hard refresh the page (Cmd+Shift+R / Ctrl+Shift+R). If still missing, try a different browser.
Hours not showing after event Wait 48 hours. If still missing, submit a support ticket with the event name and date.
Client not found in search Try searching by first name only. If still not found, add them as a new client.
Questionnaire won't submit Check for unanswered required fields (highlighted in red). Fill them in and try again.
Can't access Client Intake tab This tab is only active on event day during an active shift. Contact your coordinator to confirm you're on the roster.
