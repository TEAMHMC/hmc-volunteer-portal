FAQs

Every format on this page came from what people told us in workshops, walks, and street interviews. Here is what to expect from each one and how it was shaped by the community.

The Workshop

Power of Healing & Growth, five modules, one day.

What is the Workshop?

The Workshop is HMC's flagship in-person curriculum, recognized by LACDMH. Five healing-centered modules delivered in a single day, designed to move participants from "I do not talk about this" to "I am ready to keep talking."

It is hosted at community centers and partner clinics across LA County SPA 6 and is free for residents.

Format refined after community feedback in 2024 and 2025.
How was it built?

The curriculum was developed with mental health champions, clinicians, and community members who told us what was missing from existing programs: cultural relevance, peer voices, and time to actually feel something.

We tested early versions in small cohorts, recorded what worked, and revised the modules in response to that feedback.

Modules were updated after the Q2 2025 feedback sessions.
Who is it for?

Adults and older youth in Black and underserved communities across LA County, especially residents of SPA 6, who want a healing-centered space without clinical gatekeeping. No prior experience is needed.

The Workshop is also offered to partner organizations, schools, and faith communities that want to bring the curriculum to their own members.

How do I bring it to my community?

Partner orgs can request the Workshop through the HMC Partner Portal. We co-design the schedule, train your facilitators if needed, and share the LACDMH-aligned evaluation tools so your community sees the outcomes too.

Start a conversation at hello@healthmatters.clinic or via the Partner Portal.

Live Unstoppable

Monthly community walks and runs.

What happens at a Live Unstoppable walk or run?

A community meet-up that turns movement into medicine. Neighbors, certified mental health champions, and Crenshaw runners gather, warm up together, and move at any pace. We check in, talk if we want to, and finish together.

There is no performance pressure. The walk is the point.

The relaxed-pace format came directly from 2024 walk feedback.
Do I need to be a runner?

No. Live Unstoppable is open to all ages and paces. Walkers, joggers, runners, strollers, and folks with mobility aids are all welcome. The route always has a walk-friendly version.

When and where?

Monthly, the second Saturday at 8:00 AM. Routes rotate across Crenshaw, Leimert Park, and South LA. Specific locations and any weather updates are posted on the Event Finder.

How do I join?

Free RSVP at events.healthmatters.clinic. Bring water, comfortable shoes, and as many neighbors as you want.

Wellness Meetups

Small-group conversations on healing.

What is a Wellness Meetup?

A small-group conversation, usually 8 to 20 people, focused on a specific mental health theme. Stigma. Sleep. Grief. Strong-friend fatigue. We meet in living rooms, parks, and partner spaces. No clinical setting required.

Each Meetup is co-facilitated so no one carries the conversation alone.

Group-size cap was lowered in response to 2025 participant feedback.
How often?

Wellness Meetups happen multiple times per month across LA County, with themes rotating based on what the community is asking for. The next dates are posted on the Event Finder.

What does feedback look like?

After every Meetup we ask participants what was useful, what was missing, and what they want to talk about next. That feedback shapes future themes and the way we facilitate. The Unstoppable Report tracks these outcomes over time.

How do I host one?

Community members and partner orgs can host a Wellness Meetup through the Partner Portal. We provide the discussion guide, a trained co-facilitator, and the evaluation tools so your participants benefit from the same framework.

The Unstoppable Experience

A virtual podcast and panel series.

What is the virtual Experience?

The Unstoppable Experience is a recorded podcast and panel series where artists, athletes, clinicians, and everyday people talk about making their healing public. No scripts, no clinical jargon, and our community is the studio audience.

Conversational format was chosen after audience feedback on early panels.
Who has been on?

Guests span LA's arts, sports, and clinical communities, including mental health champions, performers, and frontline community organizers. Episodes feature both well-known voices and neighbors who have never told their story on a public platform before.

Where do I watch?

New episodes drop monthly on the HMC YouTube channel and major podcast platforms. Highlights and Shorts are shared on Instagram.

Can I be a guest?

Yes. We feature community members regularly. If you have a story you want to share, submit it through the Submit Your Story form on this page or email hello@healthmatters.clinic.
