Health Matters Clinic

Standing Orders

Version 3.0 | Effective Date: January 2026 | Approved by Medical Director

Authorization

These standing orders authorize Licensed Medical Professional volunteers of Health Matters Clinic to perform the following clinical services at HMC-sponsored community health events without requiring individual physician orders for each encounter, provided the volunteer is operating within their licensed scope of practice.

These orders are issued under the authority of the HMC Medical Director and are reviewed and updated annually or as needed based on changes to clinical practice guidelines.

Standing Order 1: Blood Pressure Screening

Protocol

  • Measure blood pressure using validated automatic or manual sphygmomanometer
  • Patient seated, rested ≥5 minutes, arm supported at heart level
  • Record systolic and diastolic pressure

Action Thresholds

ReadingCategoryAction
<120/80NormalEducate on maintenance. No referral needed.
120-129/<80ElevatedLifestyle counseling. Recommend follow-up with PCP within 3-6 months.
130-139/80-89Stage 1 HTNProvide education handout. Refer to PCP within 1 month.
140-179/90-119Stage 2 HTNProvide education. Urgent referral to PCP within 1-2 weeks.
≥180/≥120Hypertensive CrisisRecheck in 5 minutes. If persistent, refer for immediate emergency evaluation. Do not discharge without confirmed plan.

Standing Order 2: Blood Glucose Screening

Protocol

  • Use FDA-approved point-of-care glucometer
  • Clean finger with alcohol swab, allow to air dry
  • Single-use lancet only; dispose in sharps container immediately
  • Record fasting status and glucose value

Action Thresholds

ReadingStatusAction
Fasting <100 mg/dLNormalNo action. Educate on healthy lifestyle.
Fasting 100-125 mg/dLPre-diabetesProvide diabetes prevention education. Refer to PCP for confirmatory A1C.
Fasting ≥126 mg/dLDiabetes rangeRefer to PCP for diagnostic workup within 1-2 weeks.

<70 mg/dLHypoglycemiaProvide oral glucose/juice. Recheck in 15 minutes. If persistent or symptomatic, refer for emergency evaluation.

Standing Order 3: BMI Assessment

Protocol

  • Measure height (stadiometer, shoes removed) and weight (calibrated scale)
  • Calculate BMI: weight (kg) / height (m)²

Action Thresholds

<18.5UnderweightAssess nutritional status. Provide community nutrition resources.
18.5-24.9NormalEncourage maintenance of healthy weight.
25.0-29.9OverweightLifestyle counseling. Provide nutrition and physical activity resources.
≥30.0ObeseLifestyle counseling. Refer to PCP for comprehensive metabolic assessment.

Standing Order 4: OraQuick HIV Rapid Testing

Prerequisites

Volunteer must have completed the OraQuick Distribution training module in the Training Academy.

Protocol

  • Provide pre-test counseling per California Department of Public Health guidelines
  • Administer OraQuick oral swab per manufacturer instructions
  • Read results at 20 minutes (no earlier than 20, no later than 40)
  • Provide post-test counseling regardless of result

Results

  • Non-reactive: Counsel on prevention, PrEP availability, and window period
  • Reactive: Provide supportive counseling. Refer to confirmatory testing and linkage to care. Provide resource card with testing sites and hotline numbers.
  • Invalid: Re-test with new kit. If second test invalid, refer for laboratory testing.

Standing Order 5: Naloxone Distribution

Prerequisites

Volunteer must have completed the Naloxone Distribution training module in the Training Academy.

Protocol

  • Provide brief overdose recognition education (unresponsive, slow/no breathing, blue lips)
  • Demonstrate naloxone administration (nasal spray)
  • Distribute naloxone kit per California standing order for naloxone distribution
  • Document distribution: participant name (or anonymous ID), kit lot number, date
Naloxone distribution does not require a prescription under California Health & Safety Code Section 1714.22.

Standing Order 6: Emergency Response

In any life-threatening emergency, call 911 first. Then follow the protocol below.
  1. Ensure scene safety
  2. Activate EMS (call 911)
  3. Begin CPR/BLS if indicated and within your scope
  4. Use AED if available and indicated
  5. Administer naloxone if opioid overdose suspected
  6. Notify lead clinician and Clinical Coordinator
  7. Document all interventions and timeline
  8. Complete incident report within 24 hours
