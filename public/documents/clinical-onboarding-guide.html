Health Matters Clinic

Clinical Onboarding & Governance Guide

Version 1.0 | Effective Date: January 2026

1. Welcome & Mission

Welcome to Health Matters Clinic (HMC). As a Licensed Medical Professional volunteering with HMC, you play a vital role in providing free health screenings, wellness education, and community-based clinical services to underserved populations across Los Angeles County.

Our mission is to eliminate barriers to healthcare by meeting communities where they are. Your clinical expertise, combined with our operational infrastructure, enables us to deliver high-quality, culturally sensitive care at no cost to participants.

2. Organizational Structure

2.1 Clinical Governance

HMC operates under a structured clinical governance model:

  • Medical Director — Oversees all clinical operations, approves standing orders, and serves as the supervising physician for volunteer clinicians
  • Clinical Coordinator — Manages day-to-day clinical operations, scheduling, and supply chain for medical events
  • Licensed Medical Professionals — Volunteer physicians, nurses (RN/LVN), EMTs, paramedics, and allied health professionals who deliver direct patient care under standing orders
  • Core Volunteers — Non-clinical volunteers who support operations, logistics, and community engagement

2.2 Chain of Command

During clinical events, the chain of command is:

  1. Medical Director (or designated supervising physician)
  2. Clinical Coordinator / Lead Clinician on-site
  3. Licensed Medical Professional (you)
  4. Core Volunteer support staff
If you encounter a situation beyond your scope of practice, immediately escalate to the lead clinician or Medical Director.

3. Scope of Practice

All clinical volunteers must operate within their licensed scope of practice as defined by the State of California. HMC does not expand or modify your scope. You are expected to:

  • Provide services only within your professional licensure and training
  • Follow HMC standing orders for approved screening protocols
  • Decline to perform any procedure you are not qualified or comfortable performing
  • Maintain current, active licensure throughout your volunteer service

4. Clinical Event Types

4.1 Community Health Screenings

Free health screenings conducted at community events, including blood pressure checks, blood glucose testing, BMI assessments, and health risk questionnaires. All screenings follow the General Screening Consent Form protocol.

4.2 Street Medicine Outreach

Mobile clinical services delivered to individuals experiencing homelessness. Includes wound care, vital sign assessment, medication management support, and referral to permanent care. Requires completion of Street Medicine training module.

4.3 Health Fairs

Large-scale community wellness events with multiple screening stations, health education, and resource distribution. Licensed professionals may oversee multiple stations.

4.4 Wellness Education

Community workshops on topics such as chronic disease management, nutrition, mental health awareness, and preventive care. Licensed professionals may serve as subject matter experts or presenters.

5. Documentation Requirements

All clinical encounters must be documented. HMC uses a standardized documentation workflow:

  • Screening forms — Completed for every participant receiving clinical services
  • Consent forms — Signed by participants before any screening or procedure
  • Incident reports — Filed within 24 hours for any adverse events, injuries, or safety concerns
  • Shift sign-off — Completed at the end of each clinical shift via the volunteer portal

6. Infection Control & Safety

All clinical volunteers must adhere to standard precautions:

  • Hand hygiene before and after every patient contact
  • Proper use and disposal of personal protective equipment (PPE)
  • Safe handling and disposal of sharps and biohazard materials
  • Compliance with OSHA bloodborne pathogen standards
  • Immediate reporting of needlestick injuries or exposure incidents

7. HIPAA & Confidentiality

HMC is committed to protecting participant privacy. All clinical volunteers must:

  • Maintain strict confidentiality of all participant health information
  • Never discuss participant cases in public or on social media
  • Secure all paper screening forms and transfer them to the Clinical Coordinator at the end of each event
  • Report any suspected HIPAA breaches immediately to the Clinical Coordinator
Violation of HIPAA regulations may result in immediate removal from the volunteer program and potential legal consequences.

8. Emergency Protocols

In the event of a medical emergency during a clinical event:

  1. Call 911 immediately for life-threatening situations
  2. Begin appropriate first aid/BLS within your scope
  3. Notify the lead clinician and Clinical Coordinator
  4. Document the incident thoroughly using the incident report form
  5. Do not release any information to media or bystanders

9. Credentialing & Compliance

To maintain active clinical volunteer status, you must:

  • Keep your professional license current and report any changes within 72 hours
  • Upload current license, DEA (if applicable), and malpractice insurance documentation to the portal
  • Complete annual re-credentialing as prompted by the portal
  • Attend or review updated standing orders when they are issued
  • Complete any required continuing education modules assigned through the Training Academy
