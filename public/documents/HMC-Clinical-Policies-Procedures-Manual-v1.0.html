Health Matters Clinic

Clinical Policies & Procedures Manual

Version 1.0 | Effective Date: January 2026

Section 1: Patient Intake & Registration

1.1 Registration Process

All participants must complete a registration form before receiving any clinical services. Registration includes collection of basic demographic information, contact details, and a brief health history. No government-issued identification is required.

1.2 Consent Requirements

Written informed consent must be obtained from every participant before any screening or clinical service is performed. For minors (under 18), consent must be provided by a parent or legal guardian. The General Screening Consent Form (see separate document) is the standardized consent form for all routine screenings.

1.3 Language Access

HMC serves a predominantly bilingual (English/Spanish) community. All consent forms and key documents are available in both languages. Interpreter services must be provided for participants who speak other languages. Never use a minor child as an interpreter for clinical discussions.

Section 2: Screening Protocols

2.1 Blood Pressure Screening

  • Use calibrated automatic or manual sphygmomanometer
  • Patient should be seated, rested for 5 minutes, feet flat on floor, arm supported at heart level
  • Take reading on bare arm (no clothing barrier)
  • Record systolic and diastolic values
  • If reading is ≥180/120, recheck in 5 minutes. If persistent, refer for immediate evaluation
  • Provide education handout for readings ≥130/80

2.2 Blood Glucose Screening

  • Use FDA-approved point-of-care glucometer with test strips within expiration date
  • Clean participant's finger with alcohol swab, allow to dry
  • Use single-use lancet only; dispose in sharps container immediately after use
  • Record fasting/non-fasting status and glucose value
  • Fasting glucose ≥126 mg/dL or random glucose ≥200 mg/dL: refer for follow-up with primary care

2.3 BMI Assessment

  • Measure height using stadiometer (shoes removed)
  • Measure weight using calibrated scale
  • Calculate BMI using standard formula: weight (kg) / height (m)²
  • Provide education and community resources for BMI outside healthy range (18.5–24.9)

2.4 HIV Rapid Testing (OraQuick)

  • Requires completion of OraQuick distribution training module
  • Follow manufacturer instructions exactly
  • Provide pre-test counseling and post-test counseling per California guidelines
  • Reactive results: provide referral to confirmatory testing and linkage to care
  • All test kits must be tracked by lot number in the supply log

2.5 Naloxone Distribution

  • Requires completion of Naloxone distribution training module
  • Provide brief education on opioid overdose recognition and naloxone administration
  • Document distribution including participant information and kit lot number
  • Follow California standing order for naloxone distribution

Section 3: Medication & Supply Management

3.1 Supply Chain

The Clinical Coordinator is responsible for maintaining adequate supplies for all clinical events. A supply checklist must be completed before and after each event.

3.2 Medication Storage

All medications and test kits must be stored according to manufacturer specifications. Temperature-sensitive items must be transported in insulated containers with temperature monitoring.

3.3 Expiration Tracking

No expired medications, test kits, or supplies may be used. Check expiration dates as part of the pre-event supply check. Report expired items to the Clinical Coordinator for replacement.

Section 4: Incident Reporting

4.1 Reportable Incidents

The following must be reported within 24 hours using the HMC incident report form:

  • Adverse reactions to any screening or procedure
  • Needlestick or bloodborne pathogen exposures
  • Participant falls, injuries, or medical emergencies
  • Equipment malfunction during clinical use
  • HIPAA breaches or data security incidents
  • Volunteer injury during clinical service

4.2 Reporting Process

  1. Ensure immediate safety of all parties
  2. Notify lead clinician on-site
  3. Complete the incident report form (available in portal)
  4. Submit to Clinical Coordinator within 24 hours
  5. Medical Director will review all incident reports within 72 hours

Section 5: Quality Assurance

HMC conducts ongoing quality assurance reviews:

  • Monthly review of screening data and outcomes
  • Quarterly clinical competency assessments
  • Annual policy review and update
  • Post-event debriefs for all clinical events

Section 6: Infection Prevention

6.1 Standard Precautions

Standard precautions apply to all participant encounters regardless of suspected or confirmed infection status:

  • Hand hygiene with soap/water or alcohol-based hand rub before and after every encounter
  • Gloves for contact with blood, body fluids, mucous membranes, or non-intact skin
  • Face protection when splash or spray is anticipated
  • Proper waste segregation: regular waste, biohazard waste, sharps

6.2 Sharps Safety

All sharps must be disposed of in FDA-approved sharps containers immediately after use. Never recap needles. Sharps containers must be sealed and returned to HMC for proper disposal after each event.
